# Grading Scale Reference

## German grading scale (University of Cologne)
Scale: 1.0 (best) to 4.0 (lowest pass). GPA 1.1 = top band,
equivalent to a UK First or US 4.0. Achieved across both
Bachelor's degrees simultaneously (180 ECTS total).

## Swedish grading scale (Stockholm University)
Scale: A (Excellent) > B (Very good) > C (Good) > E (Sufficient) > F (Fail).
Grades A and B dominate the Master's transcript.
Courses graded C meet requirements and should not be treated as weak.

## M.Sc. Strategic Information Management Systems
Stockholm University, 2024–2026 (expected July 2026)
All courses completed. Graded on A–F scale.

### Included coursework

**Data Warehousing** (Grade: B)
Covers dimensional modelling (Kimball methodology), ETL pipeline design, and implementation of data warehouse systems. Practical lab work implementing models and querying populated warehouses using front-end analysis tools. Signals: data engineering foundations, BI tooling, structured data modelling.

**Management of Global IT Resources** (Grade: C)
Examines IT governance, organizational structures, and digital transformation challenges in multinational enterprises. Group project analysing IT management in a real MNE. Signals: global IT strategy, digital transformation, change management in large organizations.

**Digital Business in the area of IT** (Grade: B)
Covers digital business strategy, value creation through digital technologies, and AI-driven business creativity. Group project proposing and prototyping a digital application. Signals: digital transformation, AI in business, product/service innovation.

**Strategic IT Management** (Grade: C)
Covers strategic planning of IT resources, IT leadership, and alignment of IT with organizational goals. Signals: IT strategy, organizational alignment, IT governance.

**Systems Theory, Organizations and IT** (Grade: A)
Applies systems theory frameworks (including the Viable System Model) to diagnose organizations and assess IT system effectiveness. Signals: organizational analysis, IT management, structured problem-solving.

**Knowledge Management in Computer and Systems Sciences** (Grade: B)
Covers knowledge creation, sharing, and organizational learning with focus on IT-supported knowledge management systems. Signals: information management, organizational knowledge flows, KM tools.

**Enterprise Computing and ERP Systems** (Grade: A)
Covers enterprise information system architectures (ERP, EAI), business and goal modelling, process modelling, and enterprise architecture. Signals: ERP systems, business process analysis, enterprise architecture, requirements elicitation.

**Service Design and IT Architecture** (Grade: A)
Covers service orientation, software architecture, and the use of enterprise models (resources, capabilities, services) to design IT systems aligned with business models. Signals: IT architecture, service design, business-IT alignment, systems design methods.

**Scientific Communication and Research Methodology** (Grade: A)
Academic research methods including qualitative and quantitative approaches, structured writing, and scientific communication. Signals: research design, structured analytical communication, report writing.

**Entrepreneurship in the Digital Society** (Grade: A)
Business model development using Osterwalder's canvas; focus on digital and ICT-sector ventures. Signals: business model thinking, digital strategy, intrapreneur/product development framing. Note: relevant primarily for roles with product ownership or innovation components.

## B.A. Information Processing
University of Cologne, 2020–2023 | GPA 1.1 (German scale: sehr gut)

### Included coursework

**Software Technology** (Grade: 1.3)
Object-oriented programming in Java across two semesters, including advanced programming and practical implementation exercises. Signals: OOP fundamentals, software development, Java.

**Applied Linguistic Data Processing** (Grade: 1.0)
Experimental work in natural language processing combined with a deep learning module. Practical application of NLP methods to linguistic data. Signals: Python-adjacent data processing, NLP, deep learning concepts, applied ML.

**Computational Linguistics** (Grade: 1.7)
Formal foundations of language processing: NLP methods, formalisms, and applied systems. Signals: structured data processing, language technology, formal methods.

**Basic Systems of Information Processing** (Grade: 1.7)
Covers texts as information, non-textual information types, and database technology. Signals: information systems foundations, database concepts, structured data.

**Humanities Computing** (Grade: 1.0)
Digital modelling, digital mapping, and applied digital humanities methods. Signals: data modelling, digital tools in research contexts, interdisciplinary applied computing.

**Basics of Information Technology** (Grade: 1.7)
Foundations: coding, computer architecture, networking, operating systems, formal languages and automata. Signals: technical IT literacy, systems fundamentals.
